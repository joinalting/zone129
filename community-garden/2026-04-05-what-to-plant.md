# What to Plant: Tomatoes and Basil

Vivien recommends starting with tomatoes and basil, both of which thrive in Altadena's summer heat and do well in raised beds.

**Tomato varieties for our climate:**

- **Sungold** — cherry tomato, incredibly sweet, prolific producer. Great for snacking and salads.
- **Early Girl** — classic slicer, reliable in SoCal heat, produces early and keeps going.
- **Cherokee Purple** — heirloom, deep flavor, beautiful dark color. Needs a little more care.
- **Sweet 100** — cherry tomato, can yield up to 1,000 fruits per plant. Hard to beat for raised beds.

![Fresh basil in a raised bed](community-garden/attachments/basil-plant.jpg)

**Basil varieties:**

- **Genovese** — the classic Italian basil. Perfect companion to tomatoes, in the garden and on the plate.
- **Thai basil** — handles heat better than Genovese, great for Southeast Asian cooking.

Tomatoes and basil are classic companion plants. The basil helps repel pests and may improve tomato flavor. We'll discuss final selections at the first garden meeting.
