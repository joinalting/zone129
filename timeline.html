About Zone 129

What is Zone 129?

Zone 129 is a neighborhood block in Altadena, California, covering three streets: Morada Place, Mar Vista Avenue, and New York Drive. We are 117 people. Some of us have lived here for decades. Some moved in last year. After the Eaton Fire, we realized we needed a way to come together, not just to recover, but to build something lasting.

What is a Ting?

A Ting is a group of people who trust each other enough to decide together. The word comes from Old Norse, where a ting was a public assembly where free people gathered to govern themselves. Zone 129 is our Ting. It is how we organize, deliberate, and make decisions as a neighborhood.

Every Ting follows the same process: Define the question. Propose options. Discuss openly. Vote (one person, one vote). Record the decision. The record is the product: proof that we decided together.

Our Values

One Person, One Vote

Every neighbor has an equal voice. Consensus is tied to personhood, not property, seniority, or influence.

Transparency

Every decision, every discussion, every concern is recorded. The process is visible to everyone in the Ting.

Belonging

This is not a committee. It is a neighborhood. Everyone on these three streets is part of Zone 129, whether they participate actively or not.

Bottom Up

We don't wait for institutions to organize us. We organize ourselves and bring our decisions to the institutions that serve us.

Our Vision

Zone 129 is a neighborhood that governs itself. Where neighbors know each other's names, share what they have, and make decisions together about the things that affect their daily lives. We start small: a block party, a garden, a shared conversation about what this street should feel like. Each effort is practice. Each decision record is proof that we can do this.

What to Plant: Tomatoes and Basil

Vivien recommends starting with tomatoes and basil, both of which thrive in Altadena's summer heat and do well in raised beds.

Tomato varieties for our climate:

  • Sungold — cherry tomato, incredibly sweet, prolific producer. Great for snacking and salads.
  • Early Girl — classic slicer, reliable in SoCal heat, produces early and keeps going.
  • Cherokee Purple — heirloom, deep flavor, beautiful dark color. Needs a little more care.
  • Sweet 100 — cherry tomato, can yield up to 1,000 fruits per plant. Hard to beat for raised beds.

Basil varieties:

  • Genovese — the classic Italian basil. Perfect companion to tomatoes, in the garden and on the plate.
  • Thai basil — handles heat better than Genovese, great for Southeast Asian cooking.

Tomatoes and basil are classic companion plants. The basil helps repel pests and may improve tomato flavor. We'll discuss final selections at the first garden meeting.
